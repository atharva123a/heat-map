Scrollytelling with scrollama.js and d3.js (demo)

This is a scrollytelling demo. It was created using scrollama.js and d3.js. It assumes your screen is at least 1100 pixels wide and it has only been tested on a modern Firefox browser. Technical notes are down below.

Have fun.

TreeMap

A treemap is a visual method for displaying hierarchical data that uses nested rectangles to represent the branches of a tree diagram. Each rectangle has an area proportional to the amount of data it represents.

Example

Lets consider an example for TreeMap:

PET SHOP, Welcome to our pet shop! We offer a variety of adorable pets to choose from. Whether you're looking for loyal dogs, independent cats, cute mice, or friendly rabbits, we have the perfect pet for you. Explore our top-selling pets and find your new companion..

Next

At our pet shop, we have a wide range of adorable pets to choose from. Here are our top-selling pets categories: Mammals, Birds, Reptiles .

Followed By

Our Top Categaries best Selling Pets:

Highlighted part is top one: Mammals popular companions are: Dogs: Known for their loyalty and playfulness. Cats: Independent and affectionate companions. Mice: Cute and low-maintenance pets. Rabbits: friendly and great for families.

Hightlighted part is 2nd: Reptiles popular companions are: Snakes: Fascinating reptiles with various species. Geckos: Unique and easy-to-care-for lizards. Cats

Next up, Birds with Lovebirds: Colorful and social birds. Canaries Melodious singers with vibrant plumage. Cockatiels Intelligent and charming parrots.

Lastly, the treemap structure visualizes a "Pet Shop" hierarchy. At the top level is "Pet Shop," and within it, we have "Mammals and other two." Inside "Mammals," we break down into "Dogs," "Cats," "Mice," and "Rabbits," each represented proportionally in the treemap. This structure provides a clear overview of the pet shop's categories and their relationships.
